Correlation vs Covariance in Statistics

What is Covariance?

Covariance measures how two variables change together.

-   Positive covariance: both variables increase/decrease together.
-   Negative covariance: one increases while the other decreases.
-   Covariance near 0: no clear linear relationship.

Formula: Cov(X,Y) = Σ[(Xi - X̄)(Yi - Ȳ)] / (n - 1)

Limitations: - Depends on units. - Difficult to compare across datasets.

What is Correlation?

Correlation measures both the strength and direction of the
relationship.

Formula: Correlation = Cov(X,Y) / (σX × σY)

Range: - +1 : Perfect positive relationship - 0 : No linear
relationship - -1 : Perfect negative relationship

Correlation vs Covariance

  Feature      Covariance              Correlation
  ------------ ----------------------- ----------------------
  Measures     Direction               Direction + Strength
  Range        Unlimited               -1 to +1
  Unit         Depends on data units   Unitless
  Comparison   Difficult               Easy

Example

Hours Studied vs Exam Marks: - Covariance > 0: More study generally
means higher marks. - Correlation = 0.95: Strong positive relationship.

When to Use

Use Covariance to understand whether variables move together. Use
Correlation to compare relationships and measure their strength.

Interview Questions

1.  What is covariance?
2.  What is correlation?
3.  Why is correlation preferred?
4.  Can covariance be greater than 1? (Yes)
5.  Can correlation be greater than 1? (No)

Quick Revision

-   Covariance = Direction of relationship.
-   Correlation = Direction + Strength.
-   Correlation is standardized.
-   Correlation ranges from -1 to +1.
